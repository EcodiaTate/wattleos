# Creating Observations

Observations are the core of WattleOS. They document learning moments - a child choosing a particular material, completing a work cycle, collaborating with a peer, or demonstrating mastery of a concept. Creating observations is designed to be fast enough that a guide can capture a moment without interrupting the flow of the classroom.

## Who Can Create Observations

You need the **Create Observation** permission to create observations. This is included in the default Guide role. Staff with custom roles may or may not have this permission depending on how the administrator has configured their access.

## Starting a New Observation

There are three ways to start a new observation:

1. Click **New Observation** in the sidebar or on the Dashboard quick actions
2. Navigate to **Observations** and click the **New Observation** button in the top right
3. Use the keyboard shortcut **Ctrl/Cmd + N** from any page

All three take you to the observation capture form at `/pedagogy/observations/new`.

## The Capture Form

The observation form has four sections, all on a single page:

### What Did You Observe?

A free-text field where you describe what happened. Write as much or as little as needed. Some guides write a sentence ("Mia chose the pink tower and completed all ten cubes independently"), while others write detailed paragraphs about the child's approach, concentration, and self-correction. There is no minimum or maximum length.

### Students

Tag one or more students who were involved. Start typing a student's name in the search field, and matching students appear in a dropdown. Click a student to tag them. You can tag multiple students for group lessons or collaborative work.

Each tagged student appears as a coloured pill with their name and photo. Click the X on any pill to remove them.

The student search matches on first name, last name, and preferred name. If a student goes by "Mia" but is enrolled as "Amelia," searching either name will find them.

### Curriculum Outcomes

Optionally link the observation to one or more curriculum outcomes. Start typing an outcome title and matching outcomes from all active curriculum instances appear. Only outcomes and activities (the leaf-level items) appear in the search - you cannot tag high-level areas or strands directly.

Each outcome pill shows its level (outcome or activity) and title. This linking is what feeds the mastery tracking system - when you link an observation to a curriculum outcome for a student, it becomes evidence of that student's engagement with that outcome.

Linking curriculum outcomes is optional. Many observations are valuable without curriculum links, especially for social-emotional notes, transitions, or general classroom observations.

### Photos

Attach one or more photos to the observation. Click the photo button or use the file picker to select images. On iPad, this opens the camera directly so you can take a photo in the moment. On desktop, it opens the file browser.

Selected photos appear as thumbnails with individual upload status indicators. When you save the observation, each photo is compressed on your device to reduce file size, then uploaded to WattleOS storage. The status for each photo progresses through: compressing, uploading, done (or error if something went wrong).

You can remove a photo before saving by clicking the X on its thumbnail.

## Media Consent

When you attach photos to an observation that tags students, WattleOS checks each tagged student's media consent status. If any tagged student does not have media consent granted by their guardian, you will see a warning:

> **Media Consent Warning**: Media consent has not been granted for: [student names]. Photos will still be saved, but may not be shared externally.

This warning appears when you click Publish. You can choose to **Publish Anyway** (the photos are saved but flagged) or **Cancel** to go back and remove the photos or the students without consent.

Media consent is set by parents through the Parent Portal and can also be managed by administrators. A small warning icon appears next to tagged students who lack media consent whenever photos are attached to the observation.

## Saving as Draft vs Publishing

Every observation form gives you two options:

**Save as Draft** - Creates the observation in draft status. Only staff with observation permissions can see drafts. Parents cannot see drafts. Use drafts when you want to come back and add more detail, attach additional photos, or have someone review the observation before it goes to parents.

**Save & Publish** - Creates the observation and immediately publishes it. Published observations are visible in the observation feed for all staff and appear in the Parent Portal for the tagged students' guardians. You need the **Publish Observation** permission to publish (this is a separate permission from creating, so a school can require a lead guide to review and publish observations created by assistants).

If you only have the Create Observation permission but not Publish Observation, you will only see the Save as Draft button. The observation will need to be published by someone with the Publish permission.

## What Happens After Saving

After saving (draft or published), you are redirected to the observation feed. The new observation appears at the top of the list, showing its status badge (Draft or Published), the content, tagged students, linked outcomes, and media thumbnails.

The observation is now associated with your user account as the author. Only the author can edit or delete a draft observation. Published observations cannot be edited - they are a permanent record. If something needs to change, an observation can be archived and a new one created.

## Tips for Effective Observations

- **Capture in the moment**: Use the iPad camera integration to photograph the work while the child is engaged. A photo of the completed work on the shelf with the child's hands is more meaningful than a posed shot after the fact.

- **Tag multiple students**: Group lessons and collaborative work should tag all participating students. Each student's portfolio will include the observation.

- **Link to curriculum**: Even a quick link to one or two outcomes makes the observation much more valuable for mastery tracking and report generation. The search makes this fast - two or three keystrokes usually find the right outcome.

- **Write for parents**: Published observations appear in the Parent Portal. Write in a way that helps parents understand what their child is working on and why it matters. Avoid jargon where possible.

- **Draft first, publish later**: If you are in the middle of the work cycle and cannot write a full description, save a draft with just the photo and student tag. Come back during planning time to add the narrative and publish.
