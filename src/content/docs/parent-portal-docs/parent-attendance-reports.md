# Attendance and Reports

The parent portal gives you read-only access to your child's attendance records and published term reports. These sections complement the portfolio by showing the practical side of your child's school life — when they were present and what their guides have summarised about their learning each term.

## Attendance View

The attendance section shows your child's daily attendance records. Each entry includes the date, the attendance status, and (where recorded) the check-in and check-out times. Any notes added by staff for a particular day are also visible.

The possible attendance statuses are **present** (your child attended as normal), **absent** (your child did not attend), **late** (your child arrived after the expected start time), **excused** (your child was absent with an accepted reason), and **half day** (your child attended for part of the day).

At the top of the attendance view, a summary panel shows the total number of school days recorded, how many days your child was present, absent, late, excused, and attended for a half day. An overall attendance rate is calculated from the present, late, and half-day counts relative to total days.

## Weekly Snapshot

The dashboard overview card for each child includes a current-week attendance snapshot. This shows Monday through Friday of the current week with the status for each day that has been recorded so far. Days that have not yet occurred or have not been recorded appear as blank. This gives you a quick at-a-glance view without navigating to the full attendance history.

## What Parents Cannot Do

Attendance records in the parent portal are strictly read-only. Parents cannot mark attendance, modify records, or add notes. All attendance data is entered by school staff through the daily roll call or check-in/check-out system. If you believe an attendance record is incorrect, contact your child's guide or the school office to have it reviewed.

## Term Reports

The reports section shows term reports that have been published by the school. Reports go through a workflow on the staff side — they are generated from a template, filled in by the guide, reviewed, approved, and finally published. Only reports that have reached the published stage are visible in the parent portal.

Each report summary in the list shows the report term, the template name, the date it was published, and the guide who authored it. Opening a report shows the full content, which typically includes written commentary on your child's progress across different curriculum areas, social development notes, and may reference specific observations and mastery achievements from the term.

Reports are a curated summary of your child's learning journey over a term. The guide has access to all observations, mastery records, and attendance data when writing the report, so the published content represents a considered, holistic view rather than a raw data dump.

## Report PDF Downloads

Published reports can be exported as PDF documents. When available, a download link appears on the report detail page. The PDF includes the school's branding, your child's details, and the full report content formatted for printing or saving. This is useful for keeping personal records or sharing with family members who do not have portal access.

## Privacy and Access

Both attendance and report views are protected by the guardian verification system. You can only see records for children where you have an active guardian link. The data loads on the server before the page renders, and the guardian check runs on every request — there is no client-side shortcut that could expose another child's data.

Reports that are in draft, under review, or approved-but-not-published are not visible to parents. This ensures you only see final, polished reports that the school has intentionally shared.

## Permissions

No special permissions are required beyond being a registered guardian. If you can access the parent portal and see your child on the dashboard, you can view their attendance records and published reports.
