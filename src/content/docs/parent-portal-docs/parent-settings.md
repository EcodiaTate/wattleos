# Parent Settings and Consent

The settings page in the parent portal lets you manage your contact information and consent preferences for each child linked to your account. These are self-service actions — you can update them at any time without contacting the school.

## Accessing Settings

Navigate to **Settings** from the parent portal. The page shows a card for each child where you are a registered guardian. Each card displays the child's name and photo, your relationship to the child (mother, father, grandparent, etc.), and whether you are the primary contact. Below that are your contact information and consent toggles.

If you are a guardian for multiple children, each child appears as a separate card with independent settings. Consent preferences are per-child, not global — you may grant media consent for one child but not another.

## Updating Your Phone Number

Each guardian card includes a contact information section where you can update your phone number. This is the number the school uses to reach you for pickup coordination, attendance queries, and non-emergency contact. Updating it here changes the phone number stored on your guardian record, which is visible to school staff when they view your child's profile.

You do not need to contact the school to update your phone. Changes take effect immediately. This reduces administrative burden for both you and the school — previously, a parent changing their phone number might need to email the office, who would then update it in one or more systems.

## Media Consent

The media consent toggle controls whether the school can publish photos and videos of your child. When media consent is granted, observations that include photos or videos of your child can be made visible through the parent portal and used in school communications.

When media consent is not granted, guides receive a warning when they attempt to publish an observation that includes media of your child. The observation text and curriculum tags can still be published, but the school is alerted to your preference regarding photos and videos.

Media consent lives on the guardian record rather than the student record. This means different guardians for the same child can have different consent settings. However, the school typically treats consent conservatively — if any guardian has not consented, they may choose not to publish media for that child.

You can toggle media consent on or off at any time. The change is immediate and applies to future publishing decisions. It does not retroactively remove media from previously published observations.

## Directory Consent

The directory consent toggle controls whether your family's information can be included in a school directory shared with other families. When granted, basic contact information may appear in a family directory that the school makes available to its community. When not granted, your family is excluded from the directory.

Like media consent, directory consent is per-guardian and can be updated at any time. The change takes effect for future directory publications.

## What You Cannot Change

Some settings on your guardian record can only be modified by the school. Your relationship type (mother, father, grandparent, etc.), primary contact status, emergency contact designation, and pickup authorization are all managed by the school. If any of these need to change, contact your school's administration.

Your email address is tied to your WattleOS user account and is managed through your account settings, not the parent portal settings page. The settings page only covers guardian-specific information (phone and consent) that relates to your relationship with each child.

## How Settings Appear to Staff

When you update your phone number or consent preferences, the changes are immediately visible to school staff. A guide viewing your child's profile will see the updated phone number on your guardian record. Consent flags are checked whenever a guide publishes an observation with media, so your updated preference is respected on the next publishing action.

## Permissions

Parent settings are self-service. You can view and update consent preferences and contact information for any guardian record that is linked to your user account. The system verifies your identity by matching your authenticated user ID against the guardian records — you cannot modify another parent's settings, even if you are both guardians of the same child.
